🧮 How the model works — and what it's built on

1

World Football Elo

Strength comes from published Elo ratings (worldfootballrankings.com, updated 21 Jun 2026), which already absorb every result so far in this World Cup — so live form is built in objectively, not guessed.

2

Host home edge

Mexico, the USA and Canada get a calibrated Elo boost only when playing in their own country — e.g. Mexico at the Azteca, Canada at BC Place — matching the real crowd advantage hosts enjoy.

3

Opta-calibrated goals

The Elo gap sets each side's expected goals through a Poisson model tuned to the Opta supercomputer's published match odds, then read out as win/draw/loss and the most-likely exact score.

4

15,000 simulations

Every remaining game is simulated 15,000 times to estimate each nation's chance of finishing top-two or advancing as one of the eight best third-placed teams.

5

Market cross-check

Headline games show the live betting market (de-vigged moneyline) beside the model. Where they disagree on the favourite, a Model ⇄ Market split flag fires — often a sign of squad rotation in an already-decided group.

Sources: Elo strength — worldfootballrankings.com / eloratings.net (the model is anchored to the official Elo win-expectancy formula) · validated against the Opta supercomputer (theanalyst.com) and the FIFA World Ranking · market odds — bet365, DraftKings & ESPN, snapshot 20–21 Jun 2026. Fixtures, results, venues and standings verified against official FIFA group pages on 20 Jun 2026. A handful of lower-ranked nations' Elo are estimated on the same scale from their FIFA ranking. Predictions are probabilities, not guarantees — that's the fun of the World Cup.
